Liminal Arrangements

My work explores the changing colors and tones present when light filters through a space and how the changes in time reveal or conceal different elements in nature. The pieces donâ€™t attempt to show realistic representations of morning or nighttime landscapes, but rather examine the underlying moods behind natural outdoor scenes in those parts of the day. The contrasting colors call attention to the depth of space created by the tones and lines in the background and render vague forms that are in conflict with the colors around them. The use of tonal objects reminiscent of clouds and fog draws attention to the in-between, representing that which is temporary in nature rather than that which is firm and static.
